The part that never shipped

The colors were only the surface.

Beneath them sits a structured, word-level database that no other Bible platform on earth possesses — and the first app exposed only a sliver of it.

The real intellectual property

A word-level attribution dataset.

For all 66 books, human editors and linguists tagged the text at the level of the individual word — not the chapter, not the verse, the word — recording:

Who is speaking

893 distinct speakers, each with role, gender, nature, profession & chronological era.

Who is addressed

989 recipients — the audience for every spoken span of text.

In what sphere

Seven spheres of society tagged onto the words themselves.

Authored over ~40,000 hours; stored in an encrypted Emdros corpus + a relational metadata layer. Both are recovered and readable today.

The engineering that makes it portable

Metadata is anchored to
word positions, not verses.

Every word owns a permanent integer address (a “monad”). Speaker, audience, role and sphere attach to ranges of addresses. Because the anchor is positional, the same annotation layer can re-align to any translation of the text.

This is why the asset is not locked to one Bible edition — the tags describe structure, which is translation-independent.

A single record, made queryable

Everyone plays a part.

Because the tags roll up per source, every biblical figure becomes a structured profile — auto-generated, auditable, and impossible to reproduce without the underlying word-level data.

Why this wins in the AI era

Grounded by construction, not by restriction.

Every 2026 Bible-AI competitor fights the same enemy: hallucination on theological claims. Their defense is to restrict the model to cite outside commentaries.

SourceView cites the text’s own internal structure — who actually spoke, to whom, in what context — span by span. Verifiable by design, and no one else has the layer.

The multiplier

Tag once. Deploy in thousands of languages.

Because attribution is anchored to word positions rather than English wording, the layer re-attaches to any aligned translation. The 40,000-hour investment is made once — and every new translation inherits it. That turns SourceView’s biggest risk (single-translation licensing) into its biggest growth lever.
